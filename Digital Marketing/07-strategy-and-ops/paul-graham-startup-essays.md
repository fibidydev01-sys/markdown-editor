## 1️⃣ STARTUP = GROWTH (2012)

**🔗** http://paulgraham.com/growth.html

### One-Line Inti
*"A startup is a company designed to grow fast."*

### Kenapa Penting

PG kasih **definisi startup yang paling clear** dan **bedanya dari "small business"**:

> *"Being newly founded does not in itself make a company a startup. Nor is it necessary for a startup to work on technology, or take venture funding, or have some sort of 'exit'. The only essential thing is growth."*

**Implikasi praktis:**
- Lo bukan "startup" cuma karena newly founded
- Lo "startup" kalau **fundamental design lo untuk growth fast**
- Kalau lo nggak grow → lo small business (which is fine, tapi beda strategi)

### Magic Number PG
> *"The only essential thing is growth. A good growth rate during YC is 5-7% a week. If you can hit 10% a week you're doing exceptionally well. If you can only manage 1%, it's a sign you haven't yet figured out what you're doing."*

**Aturan praktis:** Kalau lo claim "startup" tapi growth <5% per minggu di early stage — lo belum nemu fit. Diagnose dulu, jangan scale.

### Aplikasi untuk Lo
- Track **weekly growth rate** explicitly (signup, MRR, active user)
- Kalau <5%/minggu di stage 0-PMF → fokus ke product-market fit, BUKAN marketing
- Compounding 5%/minggu = **12x dalam setahun**. Itu power of growth.

## 2️⃣ HOW TO START A STARTUP (2005, masih relevan)

**🔗** http://paulgraham.com/start.html

### One-Line Inti
*"You need three things: good people, something customers actually want, and to spend as little money as possible."*

### 3 Rule Inti

**1. Co-founder kuat.**
> *"What matters in a startup is product, market, and luck — in that order. But to get all three, you need good people first."*

**2. Customer real, problem real.**
> *"To make something people want, look for waves of change, then find an idea at that change wave."*

**3. Frugality.**
> *"Spend little money. Each dollar you spend extends your runway. Runway is what gives you time to make something people want."*

### Pesan untuk Founder Pemula
*"It's a mistake to focus on 'becoming a startup' rather than 'doing something people want'. Startup is just legal structure. The work is the same: make something people want."*

### Aplikasi Praktis
- Pre-fund spending: every $1 = 1 hari runway
- Co-founder lebih penting dari ide (ide bisa berubah, co-founder susah)
- Solve **real problem** lo sendiri kalau bisa (founder-market fit)

## 3️⃣ DEFAULT ALIVE OR DEFAULT DEAD? (2015)

**🔗** http://paulgraham.com/aord.html

### One-Line Inti
*"At current growth rate, would you reach profitability before running out of money?"*

### Test Sederhana yang Mengubah Cara Berpikir

PG observasi banyak founder **doesn't even know** apakah company mereka:
- **Default Alive** — Trajectory ke profitabilitas sebelum cash habis
- **Default Dead** — Cash habis duluan, butuh fundraise atau cut burn

**Test yang harus lo lakuin:**

```
Plot: Cash di tangan + monthly growth rate of revenue + monthly burn
Forecast: Apakah revenue akan exceed burn sebelum cash habis?

Kalau ya → Default Alive
Kalau enggak → Default Dead
```

Free calculator: http://growth.tlb.org

### Pesan PG ke Founder Default Dead

> *"The reason founders don't notice this distinction is that they get into the habit of thinking of fundraising as the way to make a company successful. But the metric that should matter most to them is whether their company is default alive."*

**Bahaya:** Kalau lo Default Dead dan VC market berubah jelek (kayak 2022-2024), lo mati dalam hitungan bulan.

### Aplikasi untuk Lo
- **Run the calculation hari ini**. Don't guess. Calculate.
- Default Dead OK kalau lo lagi raising — tapi lo harus tau itu
- Default Alive = leverage paling kuat saat negotiate (you don't need them)

## 4️⃣ SCHLEP BLINDNESS (2012)

**🔗** http://paulgraham.com/schlep.html

### One-Line Inti
*"The best startup ideas are hidden behind schleps that other founders avoid."*

### Apa Itu "Schlep"?

**Schlep** (Yiddish) = kerjaan yang capek, membosankan, tidak elegant. Yang nobody wants to do.

PG observasi:
> *"Most ideas that are good now were ideas back then too. The reason people don't see them is that the schleps involved keep them out of mind."*

### Contoh Schlep yang Bikin Startup Besar

- **Stripe:** Schlep = navigate banking partnerships, compliance, fraud detection. Founder lain hindari karena "too boring". Stripe embrace it → $90B valuation.
- **Plaid:** Schlep = integrasi dengan ribuan bank legacy. Capek banget. Plaid lakuin → $5B valuation.
- **Airbnb:** Schlep = convince orang asing untuk tidur di rumah orang asing. Trust building schlep. Mereka lakuin → $90B.

### Pesan PG
> *"When you find the right sort of problem, you should probably be able to describe it as obvious, at least to you. When you look at the problem in the right way, the schleps in your way evaporate into mere difficulty."*

### Aplikasi untuk Lo
- **Idea yang lo hindari karena "ribet" mungkin GOLD**
- Compete di market dimana founder lain males masuk = less competition
- Schlep early = compounding moat (susah untuk competitor catch up)

### Pertanyaan Diagnostik
*"Apa problem yang lo tau real tapi lo hindari karena 'too hard / too boring / too schlep'?"*

Jawab honest. Itu mungkin startup terbaik lo.

## 5️⃣ BEFORE THE STARTUP (2014)

**🔗** http://paulgraham.com/before.html

### One-Line Inti
*"Startups are weird. Counterintuitive. Don't trust your intuition."*

### Insight Kunci

> *"It's not enough to know intellectually that startups are counterintuitive. You actually have to act as if they are."*

PG list intuisi yang **salah** di startup:

**Intuisi salah 1:** *"Startup harus prototype dulu, baru build"*
**Reality:** *Build MVP that users actually use, learn from real usage.*

**Intuisi salah 2:** *"Strategy lebih penting dari execution"*
**Reality:** *Execution is everything. Strategy is just intent.*

**Intuisi salah 3:** *"You can plan ahead"*
**Reality:** *Most planning beyond next 90 days is fiction. Plan tactically, adapt strategically.*

**Intuisi salah 4:** *"You should commit fully now"*
**Reality:** *"Do not commit to starting a startup in college. Just learn how. Start later."*

### Pesan untuk Founder
> *"The number one thing founders ask is 'how do we know if we have a good idea?' The answer: build it and see if anyone uses it. Then talk to those users."*

### Aplikasi
- Intuition di startup = often misleading. Test reality, don't trust feel.
- Talk to user > predict user
- Build > plan

## 6️⃣ THE 18 MISTAKES THAT KILL STARTUPS (2006)

**🔗** http://paulgraham.com/startupmistakes.html

### One-Line Inti
*"The number one reason startups fail is they make something nobody wants."*

### 18 Mistake — Top 5 Paling Sering

**1. Single Founder**
Co-founder = morale + skill diversity + accountability. Solo founder = lonely + biased.

**2. Bad Location**
*"There are few startup hubs in the world. Live in one."* — Less true 2026 (remote ok) tapi proximity tetap value.

**3. Marginal Niche**
Niche kecil OK kalau valuable. Niche kecil **dan** unprofitable = trap. Validate WTP.

**4. Derivative Idea**
*"X but for Y" sering jadi weak. Originality matters.*

**5. Obstinacy**
Stick to bad plan stubbornly. **Pivot when data tells you to.**

### Mistake #1 BESAR yang Underrated: "Making Something Nobody Wants"

> *"It's so easy to attribute startup failure to lack of execution. But the most common reason is just: nobody wanted what they made."*

**Test:**
- 10 user terakhir lo: apakah mereka **upset** kalau produk lo ilang besok?
- Sean Ellis PMF test: >40% "very disappointed" if no longer use?
- Word-of-mouth: ada nggak?

Kalau jawaban "no" untuk 3 di atas → **lo lagi build sesuatu yang nobody wants**.

### Aplikasi
Run Sean Ellis PMF survey **bulan ini**. Honest interpretation. Pivot kalau hasil <40%.

## 7️⃣ HOW NOT TO DIE (2007)

**🔗** http://paulgraham.com/die.html

### One-Line Inti
*"If you can just avoid dying, you get rich."*

### Logic Brutal PG

Mayoritas startup mati. Kalau lo **sekedar nggak mati**, lo otomatis di top 10%.

**3 cara nggak mati:**

**1. Manage money well.**
> *"Spending is the easiest way to die. Don't spend until you have to."*

**2. Stay determined.**
> *"The most common mistake is to give up too early."*

**3. Don't be in denial.**
> *"If something's wrong, you have to face up to it."*

### Insight Kunci untuk Founder

> *"In a startup, the biggest danger is to your own morale, not your investors' or competitors'."*

**Translasi:**
- Founder yang **stop trying** = startup yang dies.
- Founder yang **keep going** despite slow growth = often eventually succeed.

PG cerita banyak company yang **looked dead** for 2-3 tahun, lalu suddenly broke through.

### Aplikasi untuk Lo
- Money: budget conservatively, treat every dollar like last
- Morale: protect founder morale aggressively (cofounder, advisor, founder community)
- Honesty: face reality. Pivot kalau perlu, but don't quit prematurely

## 8️⃣ THE HARDEST LESSONS FOR STARTUPS TO LEARN (2006)

**🔗** http://paulgraham.com/startuplessons.html

### One-Line Inti
*"Startups must do things that don't pay off immediately."*

### 7 Lesson Inti

**Lesson 1: Release early.**
> *"Get a version 1 out fast, then improve it based on users' reactions."*

**Lesson 2: Keep pumping out features.**
> *"As long as you're working steadily on releases, you're sending the right message: this product is alive, this company is competent."*

**Lesson 3: Make users happy.**
> *"You should literally minimize the number of unhappy users."*

**Lesson 4: Fear the right things.**
> *"Don't worry about competitors. Worry about indifference."*

**Lesson 5: Commitment is a self-fulfilling prophecy.**
> *"Founders who commit fully tend to succeed. Founders who hedge tend to fail."*

**Lesson 6: There is always room.**
> *"Even in saturated markets, there's room for products that are 10% better."*

**Lesson 7: Don't get demoralized.**
> *"The day-to-day is grinding. Stay anchored to the long view."*

### Aplikasi
Ship release **weekly** (atau biweekly). Public changelog. Cadence shows aliveness.

## 9️⃣ MEAN PEOPLE FAIL (2014)

**🔗** http://paulgraham.com/mean.html

### One-Line Inti
*"Being mean makes you stupid."*

### Counter-Intuitive Insight

PG observation di YC: founder mean (asshole) **lebih sering gagal** dari founder nice.

> *"Mean founders are less likely to ask for help, because they don't trust others. They're less likely to learn from others, because they don't respect others. They're less likely to attract good people, because good people don't want to work with mean people."*

### Pesan untuk Founder
- Be **demanding** dengan quality? Yes.
- Be **harsh** dengan execution? OK in measure.
- Be **mean** sebagai default behavior? Kills you long-term.

### Practical Wisdom
- Treat employees, customers, partners dengan respect
- Long game wins; mean people optimize short-term di expense long-term
- Reputation compounds (positive or negative)

### Aplikasi
- Audit lo sendiri: kapan terakhir lo mean ke karyawan/customer? Why?
- Build culture of demanding + respect. Not mean.

## 🔟 THE 4 QUADRANTS OF CONFORMISM (2020)

**🔗** http://paulgraham.com/conformism.html

### One-Line Inti
*"Independent-mindedness is the most important quality in a founder."*

### 4 Quadrant PG

PG plot orang di 2 dimensi:
- **Aggressively independent-minded** vs **passively independent-minded**
- **Aggressively conventional-minded** vs **passively conventional-minded**

**Best founder profile:**
- **Aggressively independent-minded** — Question every assumption, challenge conventional wisdom, comfortable being contrarian

**Why it matters:**
Startup yang berhasil **counter-intuitive by definition** (kalau intuitive, big company udah lakuin).

> *"To do exceptional work, you have to think things that other people don't think."*

### Practical: Cara Develop Independent Mind
- Question conventional wisdom in your domain
- Read first-principles thinking (Naval, Munger, Bezos)
- Surround yourself dengan independent thinkers
- Reduce social media consumption (conformity pressure)
